6. Interpretation Notes

Evidence for declension:

  • Adjacent-word suffix agreement is 1.4вЂ“2.0Г— above chance (2-char and 3-char endings)
  • 71вЂ“76% of frequent words show free positional distribution (high entropy)
  • Vowel doubling (i/ii, e/ee) produces forms with different syntactic contexts, not random variation вЂ” collocation Jaccard often <0.2
  • Same stems appear in both Currier languages with systematically different ending preferences, consistent with two dialects sharing a morphological system
  • The number of productive paradigms (200вЂ“400 stems Г— 3+ endings) is consistent with an agglutinative or fusional language

Caveats:

  • Case labels (NOM?, ACC?, etc.) are purely positional heuristics вЂ” true identification requires semantic decipherment
  • "Stems" and "endings" are statistical artifacts until the script is decoded; the real morpheme boundaries may differ
  • The high hapax rate (68% of types) could indicate productive morphology or a cipher with variable encoding
