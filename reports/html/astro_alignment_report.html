6. Synthesis and Hypotheses

Key structural observations

  1. The o- prefix on star labels: 85%+ of star labels begin with o. The Arabic definite article al- begins virtually every Arabic star name. This is the single strongest structural correspondence. If o = /al/, the phonetic mapping would be: the two-phoneme article /al/ collapsed to a single EVA glyph.
  2. The lk- astronomical prefix: Almost exclusive to star pages. Structural parallel to Turkic Гњlker (Pleiades) вЂ” but given its frequency (45+ occurrences), it's more likely a functional word ("star", "constellation"?) rather than a proper name. Compare Turkic yulduz or Arabic manzil.
  3. Zodiac label morphology: The zodiac labels show the same o- prefix + stem + suffix structure as the declension system. Labels like otalchy, otalalg, otaraldy share stems with suffixed variants вЂ” these may be zodiac sign names in their declined forms (e.g., genitive "of Taurus", locative "in Aries").
  4. Label length distribution: Voynich star labels average 6-8 EVA characters. Arabic star names (minus article) average 4-7 characters. The slight excess in Voynich labels is consistent with a suffixed/agglutinative encoding (stem + case/declension ending).
  5. The "women with pots" = stars hypothesis: If the nymph/woman figures represent stars and the pots/vessels represent magnitude or constellation membership, this aligns with the Islamic astronomical tradition where stars are described by their position in a figure (e.g., "the heart of the scorpion", "the foot of the giant"). The labels would then be the names of individual stars within each figure.
